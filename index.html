Agent 01 Live

Audit Perspectives

A STORM-style (Shao et al., NAACL 2024), multi-stakeholder method for audit questions. It runs any audit question through every layer of an engagement team — Staff through Partner, Core Audit through IT, Tax, and Advisory — plus external stakeholders like the PCAOB, AICPA, regulators, and investors.

Then it maps where the views conflict and synthesizes a practical recommendation specific enough to act on.

Method

How these agents are built

Four rules, applied to every agent in this collection. They're what separate a tool an auditor can rely on from a demo that falls apart on real data.

01

Multi-perspective by design

Real engagements are arguments between viewpoints — Staff and Partner, preparer and reviewer, firm and regulator. Every agent models the disagreement, not just the answer, because the disagreement is where the risk lives.

02

Deterministic before generative

Wherever the input is data, findings come from computed diagnostics — contra balances, unreconciled clearing accounts, equity that doesn't roll — and the language model interprets them. No perspective gets to claim what the numbers don't show.

03

Evidence behind every finding

Auditors don't accept conclusions without support, and neither do these agents. Every finding cites its source — an account and amount, a standard, a stated perspective — so the output can survive review.

04

Shipped open, documented fully

Each agent publishes its full working instructions — the actual contract it runs on — so anyone in the profession can inspect the method, challenge it, or adapt it to their own practice.
