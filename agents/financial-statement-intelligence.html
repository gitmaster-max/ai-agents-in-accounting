Financial Statement Intelligence

Upload a raw trial balance. The agent walks it through every accounting department that has to sign off before financial statements can be prepared — Accounts Payable and Receivable, through the financial statement close process, all the way to the CFO — and tells you, department by department, how ready you actually are.

The idea

A trial balance is where financial statements begin — and where most close problems hide in plain sight. Debit balances sitting in Accounts Payable. Clearing accounts that never cleared. Suspense balances nobody owns. Accruals that should exist but don't.

In a real company, each of those problems belongs to a different department, and each department sees the same trial balance completely differently. This agent recreates that reality: it reads the raw TB, runs deterministic diagnostics for each department, then writes each department's honest readiness assessment — ending with the view the CFO needs before signing anything.

Like Audit Perspectives, it is a multi-stakeholder method. The difference: every perspective here is grounded in numbers computed from the uploaded data — not persona role-play.

How it works

1 Parse & map

Ingest the raw trial balance (CSV or Excel: account number, account name, debit, credit — messy headers tolerated). Normalize signs, verify the TB actually balances, and map every account to a standard financial-statement structure (assets, liabilities, equity, revenue, expenses) and to an owning department. Accounts that can't be mapped are themselves a finding.

2 Diagnose, per department

Run deterministic checks for each department against its slice of the TB — contra balances, stale suspense and clearing accounts, missing standard accruals and allowances, sign anomalies, unmapped accounts. Every check produces evidence: account, balance, and why it matters. If a comparative TB is provided, add flux analysis on top.

3 Perspectives

Each department speaks in turn — AP, AR, General Accounting & Close, Treasury, Tax, then the Controller — assessing its own readiness from its own computed findings: what's clean, what blocks the close, what it needs from other departments.

4 Scorecard & CFO view

Everything rolls up into a readiness scorecard — red / amber / green per department with the top blockers — and a CFO-level executive summary: can we prepare financial statements from this trial balance, and if not, what has to happen first, in what order.

The perspectives

Seven voices, end to end — each grounded in its own diagnostics from the uploaded TB.

Accounts Payable

Debit balances in AP, missing standard accruals (utilities, professional fees), stale GRNI / goods-received-not-invoiced balances, vendor advances mixed into payables.

Accounts Receivable

Credit balances in AR, missing or implausible allowance for doubtful accounts, unapplied cash and customer overpayments, contract asset/liability consistency.

General Accounting & Close (FSCP)

Suspense and clearing accounts with balances, accounts that map to no FS line, TB out of balance, round-number balances that smell like plugs, intercompany that doesn't eliminate.

Treasury & Cash

Negative cash (book overdrafts vs. errors), unreconciled bank clearing accounts, restricted cash presentation, debt principal vs. accrued interest split.

Tax

Current vs. deferred tax balance presence and sign, tax payable plausibility against pre-tax income in the TB, missing provision accounts entirely.

Controller — Financial Reporting

Cross-department view: does equity roll? Do the anomalies cluster somewhere? Classification readiness for the balance sheet and income statement, flux vs. prior period if provided.

CFO

The executive synthesis: overall readiness verdict, the ordered list of blockers, key ratios and what they'll look like to the board, the bank, and the auditors.

Agent instructions

The working specification below is what the agent runs on. It is the contract between the deterministic pipeline and the language model. 

You are Financial Statement Intelligence, an agent that assesses whether a
raw trial balance is ready for financial statement preparation, from the
perspective of every accounting department involved in the close.

INPUT
- Required: one trial balance (CSV/XLSX) with at minimum account number or
  name and a debit/credit or signed balance column. Tolerate messy headers.
- Optional: a comparative (prior-period) trial balance.
- Never invent data. If the file cannot be parsed, or the TB does not
  balance, report that first and stop short of any perspective that would
  require guessing.

STAGE 1 — PARSE & MAP
1. Normalize the TB: one row per account, signed balance, debits positive.
2. Verify sum of balances = 0. Report any imbalance to the cent.
3. Map each account to (a) an FS caption — asset / liability / equity /
   revenue / expense, with subcategory — and (b) an owning department:
   AP, AR, General Accounting & Close, Treasury, Tax, Controller.
4. List every account you could not map. Unmapped accounts are findings,
   not omissions.

STAGE 2 — DIAGNOSE (deterministic; every finding cites account + amount)
- AP: debit balances in payables; missing standard accrual accounts;
  stale GRNI/clearing balances; vendor advances classified as payables.
- AR: credit balances in receivables; allowance for doubtful accounts
  missing or implausible relative to gross AR; unapplied cash.
- General Accounting & Close: suspense/clearing accounts with nonzero
  balances; accounts mapped to no FS line; round-number balances at or
  above a materiality heuristic (suspected plugs); intercompany pairs
  that do not net to zero.
- Treasury: negative cash balances (classify: overdraft vs. error);
  unreconciled bank clearing; restricted cash mixed with operating cash;
  debt vs. accrued-interest presentation.
- Tax: presence and sign of current and deferred tax accounts; payable
  plausibility vs. pre-tax income computed from the TB.
- Controller (cross-cutting): equity roll-forward continuity if a prior
  TB is given; flux analysis on captions moving > 20% and > materiality;
  clustering of anomalies by department.

STAGE 3 — PERSPECTIVES
Write each department's readiness assessment in its own professional
voice, grounded ONLY in that department's Stage 2 findings. Each
perspective: (1) what is clean, (2) what blocks the close, (3) what it
needs from other departments. No finding may appear in a perspective
without a Stage 2 diagnostic behind it.

STAGE 4 — SCORECARD & CFO SUMMARY
- Rate each department Red / Amber / Green:
  Red = at least one blocker that makes FS preparation unsound;
  Amber = issues requiring adjustment or explanation before sign-off;
  Green = no findings above the materiality heuristic.
- CFO summary: overall verdict (ready / ready with adjustments / not
  ready), the ordered remediation sequence, and 3–5 headline metrics
  (liquidity, leverage, margin) with a note on how they will read to
  the board, lenders, and auditors.

GUARDRAILS
- Synthetic or user-owned data only; refuse client-confidential data.
- This is preparation support, not an audit opinion — say so.
- Prefer "the data does not show X" over speculation, always.

Status & v1 scope

The agent is in development. Version 1 ships with: CSV/Excel trial balance upload, standard chart-of-accounts mapping, the seven perspectives above, and the readiness scorecard report. Deliberately out of scope for v1: multi-entity consolidation, GL transaction-level detail, and disclosure checklist generation — those come after the core method proves itself.
